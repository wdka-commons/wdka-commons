Free Websites & Tools

Accessing and sharing knowledge outside institutional systems often requires alternative digital tools and platforms. For students and artists without stable academic affiliations, these tools enable research, learning, and publishing beyond paywalls, surveillance, and platform restrictions.

Some tools focus on privacy, circumvention, or decentralisation, allowing users to access blocked or restricted materials and communicate outside mainstream platforms. Others function as aggregators, indexes, or community-maintained guides that lower the threshold for finding resources and navigating informal infrastructures of access.

While the use of such tools can raise legal and ethical questions, their widespread adoption highlights the structural conditions that make them necessary — particularly within education systems shaped by enclosure, rising costs, and institutional gatekeeping.

These tools should be understood not as neutral utilities, but as part of a broader ecosystem of mutual aid, self-education, and collective knowledge sharing.
